Brand Activation Kiosk

Páginas Amarelas Muppi

Designing a touch-screen kiosk experience for a live brand activation, turning concert-goers into brand leads through music, personalisation, and a physical keepsake.

Role UX/UI Designer
Company New Normal
Type Brand activation · Live event
Presence 4 concerts
4 concerts where the kiosk was present
~3k posters printed across all events
Queue formed spontaneously at every event
The challenge

Capturing attention in the middle of a concert

Páginas Amarelas wanted a brand activation that felt memorable, not like a lead generation exercise. The challenge was designing a kiosk experience that people would genuinely want to use, in a loud, busy concert environment, with a queue forming behind them.

No time for formal research, the experience had to be intuited from context and rapid iteration. Concert environment: loud, emotional, people in a hurry. No room for confusion or long flows. Two goals in one: collect emails for the brand and create a personal, emotional moment for the user, simultaneously.

The process

Designing within the context

With a queue forming, each interaction had to be completable in under a minute. Every unnecessary step was a reason to abandon. Kiosk format means no keyboard, no mouse, no sitting down, touch targets had to be large, labels instant to read, and the flow had to work with one hand.

Concert-goers are in a heightened emotional state. The experience had to match that energy, personal, warm, and connected to the music they'd just heard. The printed poster was the reward, and someone else designed it. The kiosk flow had to set up that handoff cleanly, collecting the right information without friction.

The experience was designed as a linear, progressive flow: enter email → choose favourite song → pick a lyric to dedicate → write who it's for → enter the meet & greet draw → print poster. The meet & greet draw was positioned at the end, after the user had already invested emotionally. By that point, leaving an email felt like a natural next step, not a data capture exercise.

The results

People queued to use it

Every screen had one clear action, no branching paths, no optional steps mid-flow. Song selection as the emotional hook: choosing a favourite song was the moment the experience became personal, designed to feel like a music app, not a form. Lyrics offered as pre-written options, fast, on-brand, and emotionally resonant without the friction of a touch keyboard.

The best signal wasn't a number, it was the queue. People chose to wait to use this at a concert, when they could have been anywhere else. ~3,000 posters printed across 4 events. That's the real measure of a kiosk experience working.

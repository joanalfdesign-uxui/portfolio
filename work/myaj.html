Redesigning a loyalty app used by car workshop staff across a business group, reducing transaction errors from 77.6% to zero.

Role Product Designer
Company Innovaj
Type Internal product · Redesign
Timeline ~6 months
77.6% transaction error rate before redesign
0% incompatible transactions after redesign
100% of errors now caught and correctable in real time
The challenge

A system breaking down in real time

The My AJ loyalty app was already live and in daily use across the group's car workshops. Receptionists used it to register sales, apply coupons, and sync transactions with the billing system. The problem: 77.6% of those transactions were flagged as incompatible — a mismatch between what the app recorded and what the billing system expected.

The challenge wasn't to build something new. It was to understand why a working tool was failing the people using it, and fix it without breaking their workflow.

The process

Understand first, redesign second

The research was conducted on-site, not in a lab. 10 user interviews and 6 store visits, watching receptionists use the app in the middle of real working days, with customers waiting and phones ringing. 80% of staff didn't know they could apply more than one coupon per transaction. 75% needed immediate feedback to know if a transaction succeeded. 50% of observed actions showed the interface didn't match the natural workflow.

Before designing anything, I mapped every screen of the live app against the billing system's logic, identifying exactly where the data diverged. The invoice ID step was the critical failure point: users had to manually enter a number they often couldn't locate on the physical invoice. The root cause wasn't user error, it was a design that assumed knowledge users didn't have, in a context that left no room for trial and error.

I redesigned the full transaction flow, from home screen to confirmation, aligning the app's logic with how staff actually worked. After launch, a short workshop was run with staff to walk through the new flow and explain how to read invoices correctly, closing a knowledge gap that no interface change alone could fix.

The results

From 77.6% errors to zero

QR code scan replaced manual invoice ID entry, immediate confirmation that app data matches billing data. A persistent message now confirms discounts applied and explicitly invites adding more. Clear labelling at every step eliminated ambiguity about transaction status. Immediate error feedback at the point of entry means users can correct mistakes on the spot, before the customer leaves.

The 0% error rate was measured at launch. Ongoing tracking is needed to ensure the improvement holds as new staff onboard and edge cases emerge. Formalising the invoice literacy training into onboarding for new receptionists would prevent the problem from resurfacing.

Next steps

What comes next

Monitor error rates over time, the 0% result was measured at launch, but ongoing tracking is needed to ensure the improvement holds as new staff onboard and edge cases emerge. Expand the workshop training into a formal onboarding process so new receptionists have the invoice literacy they need from day one.

The challenge

The problem worth solving

In 2024, FEUP opened applications for Isolated Course Units — letting candidates deepen knowledge without enrolling in a full degree. There was no system to handle it. Academic staff processed everything via spreadsheets, placement was done by hand, and candidates had no visibility into anything.

Three core problems shaped the brief: applications managed entirely via spreadsheets with no digital system; placement rules applied by hand with no automation or consistency; and zero transparency for candidates — no status tracking, no timeline, no self-service.

The process

How we designed it

The system had to serve two very different users: Maria, a 26-year-old UP candidate already in the system who logs in via SIGARRA and has her academic data retrieved automatically; and Leonardo, a 34-year-old external candidate with no prior account who needs to manually input his academic history. Same flow, fundamentally different starting points.

We started by mapping tasks for both candidate types and the academic team, identifying where friction was most likely. A key dependency shaped everything: candidate progress relied on academic services validation before placement could run.

Low-fi wireframes were built in Excel — intentionally. The internal dev team at FEUP gave faster, higher-quality feedback there than in Figma or Miro. It kept alignment tight with the people building the system.

The prototype was tested with 4 participants matching the target profile. Task completion time was deliberately tracked — because submission time was one of the placement algorithm's criteria. Average task completion: ~4 min, excluding academic history upload. No critical issues were found. The step-by-step progress indicator consistently reduced user hesitation.

The results

Built from zero, shipped in weeks

The platform covered the full journey — landing page with key dates and FAQs, a dashboard for tracking applications, a step-by-step flow for applying, selecting and ordering course units by preference, reviewing before submitting, and checking results after the placement period closed.

Candidates with academic history were guided through all required fields. Those without could still apply. Either way, the system adapted and communicated clearly what came next. Results were delivered by email and via dashboard; enrolment and payment happened directly in the platform.

The system launched with no prior version to compare against. 59% of all applications came in on Day 1 — and 89% were correctly submitted and validated without manual intervention.

Next steps

Room to improve

Investigate the external candidate gap. 1,165 UP applications vs 44 external — too large to ignore. Research needed to understand if it's a demand issue or a UX friction point.

Refine system messages. Email, SMS, and in-app notifications should be reviewed for tone, timing, and clarity — particularly around results and required next actions.

Retrospective

"With more time, I would have tested the full flow — including the academic history upload, the heaviest step and the one most likely to cause drop-off. I'd also explore how to communicate the role of submission time in placement without creating unnecessary urgency."
